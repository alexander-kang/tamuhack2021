Intro

Facts:

  • circleEvery year, households waste 76 billion pounds of food
  • circleIn total, Americans waste $161 billion each year on food
  • circleThe average person wastes 238 pounds of food each year (that's 21% of the food they buy!)
  • circleAn average family of four wastes $1600 a year on produce alone
  • circleOnly 5% of food is composted, making food waste one of the largest components of trash in landfills
  • circleIn landfills, food waste breaks down to produce methane & other greenhouse gases
  • circleFood waste is responsible for 25% of all freshwater consumption & is one of the leading causes of freshwater pollution

*All data collected in the U.S.*

Learn more:

  • circleFoodPrint - The Problem of Food Waste
  • circleRTS - Food Waste in America in 2020

About

What do we do and what can we offer you?

Here, at Green Savings, we're dedicated to helping America's households build their financial security in a green way.

  • 01Increasing the financial security of America's households

    By reducing the 238 pounds of wasted food for an individual every year, we enable households & individuals to save money on groceries to save for the future or spend on other essential purchases. A family of four can expect their purchase from us to pay for itself in around a year; from then on, all savings can be considered positive returns on investment.

  • 02Keeping our planet healthy

    By reducing the amount of wasted food going to landfills, we're not only taking away one of the largest contributors to landfill garbage, but we're also reducing the number of greenhouse gases being released from decomposing food in landfills. Additionally, we can keep our lakes & rivers clean from the harsh chemicals, waste, plastic, etc. that come along with freshwater pollution.
